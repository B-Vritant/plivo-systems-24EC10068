To satisfy the strict 1% deadline miss cap and the 2.0x bandwidth overhead limit, this solution utilizes a Hybrid FEC (Forward Error Correction) + ARQ (Automatic Repeat Request) architecture. This design treats network unreliability as a mathematical constraint, optimizing for the lowest possible microsecond-level latency.

1. The Sender (State, Parity & Retransmission)

The sender continuously monitors the harness input (port 47010) and the feedback channel (port 47004). Outbound frames are instantly forwarded and logged into an std::map history buffer capped at 100 entries.

To preemptively combat packet loss, the sender executes a pairwise bitwise XOR parity protocol. For every odd-numbered sequence, it generates a parity packet (flagged via the highest bit: seq | 0x80000000) containing the XOR payload of the current and previous frame. If a NACK is received, the sender falls back to ARQ and instantly retransmits the requested sequence from its history map.

2. The Receiver (Instant Reconstruction & Gap Detection)

The receiver bypasses traditional, artificially delayed jitter buffers. New, in-order packets are forwarded directly to the player (port 47020) the microsecond they arrive.

If a sequence gap is detected, the receiver attempts instant FEC recovery. If one frame of a pair drops but the sibling frame and the parity packet arrive, the missing 160-byte payload is mathematically reconstructed (Frame = Sibling ^ Parity) and played without incurring round-trip delay. If the parity packet is also lost, the receiver immediately constructs a 4-byte NACK for the missing sequence and fires it upstream (port 47003).
